
Adidas, a global leader in sportswear and lifestyle products, operates across multiple countries through retail stores, franchise outlets, 
and e-commerce platforms. With intense competition from brands like Nike and Puma, along with rising customer expectations in digital shopping
experiences, retaining customers has become a critical challenge. Although Adidas collects vast data on customer purchases, online interactions, and loyalty programs, their current reporting lacks the analytical depth to:

The Goal and Objective is too

a. Understand why customers are churning?
b. Identify loyal vs. at-risk customers
c. Measure the impact of loyalty tiers, promotions, and influencer-driven campaigns
d. Guide region- and channel-specific retention strategies

Develop a robust, interactive Customer Retention Analytics Dashboard in Power BI using
Adidas data that will:
1. Consolidate customer demographics, purchase history, store/e-commerce performance,
and loyalty data
2. Enable dynamic segmentation of high-value, repeat, and churned customers
3. Provide actionable insights to improve customer retention, loyalty engagement, and
regional strategies

Datasets used

1. Customer_Demographics: Contains demographic and membership details for Adidas customers.
 Customer_ID, Age, Gender, Region, Income_Level, Membership_Since, Preferred_Channel (Store/Online)

2. Customer_Transactions: Logs purchase transactions across Adidas retail stores, franchise outlets, and online platforms.
   Transaction_ID, Customer_ID, Store_ID, Product_Category (Footwear, Apparel, Accessories), Transaction_Date, Amount, Promotion_Applied

3. Store_Locations: Metadata about Adidas retail and franchise locations. Store_ID, Store_Type (Flagship, Franchise, Outlet, Online), Region,
Opening_Year.

4. Loyalty_Program: Tracks customer participation in Adidas’ Creators Club loyalty program. Customer_ID, Loyalty_Tier (Base, Plus, Premium, Elite), Points_Earned, Points_Redeemed.

5. Churn_Labelled_Customers.csv: Provides a churn flag based on last purchase date and
behavioral indicators. Customer_ID, Last_Purchase_Date, Churn_Flag (0 = Active, 1 = Churned), Churn_Reason (Inactivity, Competitor, Low Engagement)

Tasks performed-
Step 1- Data Modeling & Cleaning
Step 2- Churn & Retention Metrics 
Step 3- Repeat Purchase Analysis 
Step 4- Promotion & Loyalty Impact
Step 5- Store & Channel Performance vs Retention 
Step 6- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) Analysis 
Step 7- Final Dashboard & Executive Summary
